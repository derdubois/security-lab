Summary: LAN Topologies
Networks can be designed in different shapes called topologies. Here are the three main ones:
Star Topology — All devices connect to a central switch/hub. Most common today because it's reliable and scalable, but more expensive and if the central device fails, everything goes down.
Bus Topology — All devices share one backbone cable. Cheap and easy to set up, but slow under heavy traffic and has no redundancy — one cable break kills the whole network.
Ring Topology — Devices are connected in a loop, passing data around until it reaches the destination. Easy to troubleshoot and less prone to bottlenecks, but a single cut cable or broken device takes down the entire network.
Switches vs Hubs — Switches are smarter than hubs; they track which device is on which port and send data only to the intended device, reducing unnecessary traffic.
Routers — Connect different networks together and find the best path for data to travel between them.
Connecting switches and routers together adds redundancy — if one path fails, data can take another route, keeping the network up.
